Abstract

Implicitly trusted as the immutable ground truth of digital reality, screenshots faithfully capture the exact state of a user interface. This reliability renders them indispensable across diverse workflows. However, we reveal that this trusted bridge has fractured due to a widening technological decoupling between high dynamic range (HDR) display hardware and legacy screenshot software (e.g., sRGB). We uncover a critical vulnerability: visual signals rendered in the extended range are irreversibly flattened during capture. Building on this, we propose the Mirage Attack, which exploits this discrepancy to deceive both AI agents and human users. Our evaluation demonstrates significant threats to GUI Agents and online interview integrity, while also proposing a novel defensive application: MirageCAPTCHA.

Mechanism

The vulnerability stems from the rigid 8-bit integer pipeline of legacy screenshot subsystems (GDI, CoreGraphics) which are calibrated to the sRGB standard. Modern displays supporting Rec.2020 and high nit brightness render content that exists outside this captured range.

Implementation Challenges

  • (C1) Visual Consistency: We devise Dual Display Modes (Bright Mode & Dark Mode) to ensure the hidden content blends seamlessly into the UI on the physical screen while vanishing in the capture.
  • (C2) Hardware Heterogeneity: We introduce the Mirage Adaptation mechanism to approximate the unknown tone-mapping functions of diverse displays.

Impact Analysis

Threat to AI Agents

We evaluated 5 GUI Agents (e.g., ChatGPT CUA) and 2 Commercial Browser Agents. The attack caused task success rates to drop precipitously (e.g., from 84.2% to 11.7% for GUI Agents), forcing agents into prolonged reasoning loops due to conflicting visual data.

Deception in Human Workflows

In our MirageCheating study, candidates used the attack to view cheat sheets during interviews. The hidden content captured by the screenshot tool was blank, rendering the cheating behavior virtually undetectable to remote interviewers.

Defensive Application: MirageCAPTCHA

By inverting the attack logic, we created MirageCAPTCHA. It presents challenges that are visible to humans on HDR screens but invisible to multimodal AI solvers (e.g., GPT-4o, Claude Sonnet).
